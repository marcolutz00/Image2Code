Update your user macros on Confluence 5.5.1 and atlassian-xwork-core-1.17

Written by David Simpson on May 22, 2014. Back to the start. Topics:

Following yesterday's Atlassian security warning regarding Confluence, it seems additional steps are necessary for users with user macros. Take care.

@BergerofLondon reported this initially:

After upgrading to Confluence 5.5.1, parameter values in user macros are evaluated differently. Found this out the hard way

— Berger (@BergerofLondon) May 22, 2014

Shorthand for standard variables won’t work. #if ($paramistrue) will now always be interpreted as true.

— Berger (@BergerofLondon) May 22, 2014

Instead, use patterns such as #if ($paramistrue == true) or #if ($paramistrue == false).

— Berger (@BergerofLondon) May 22, 2014

@baitman String testing requires #if ($paramfoo).length() > 0 instead of #if ($paramfoo). Absurd!

— Berger (@BergerofLondon) May 22, 2014

@baitman When updating to 5.5.1 for security, confirm xwork is also upgraded to 1.17. It wasn't in our case.

— Berger (@BergerofLondon) May 22, 2014

The atlassian-xwork-core-1.17.jar upgrade mandates more explicit code. Many thanks to @BergerofLondon for alerting us.
