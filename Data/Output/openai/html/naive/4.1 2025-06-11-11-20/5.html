Bypassing Restrictions to Upload to
YouTube

July 25, 2007
Dave, employed at a prominent company, recently shared via a social network that he couldn't share video recordings to YouTube as his employer blocks access to YouTube and similar video hosting sites through the corporate firewall.

One way to solve this is to use your mobile device to put content on YouTube - set up a mobile account on YouTube from a computer outside the firewall and then send videos to YouTube through MMS.

See Also: How to Unblock Restricted Sites

Another method is using a multi-platform uploader - use a service to upload your video to numerous websites at once.

You would upload your video to this other service, and they would then upload it to many sites such as YouTube, MySpace, PhotoBucket, MetaCafe, Google, Yahoo! video, DailyMotion, etc.

It's likely the other video hosting service is available - they will notify you by email when your upload completes.
